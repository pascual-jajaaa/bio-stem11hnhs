Types of Connective Tissues

Connective Tissue Proper

Includes both loose connective tissue and dense connective tissue.
Loose connective tissue has a higher amount of ground tissue as compared to fibers.
Dense connective tissue has higher amount of fibers compared to ground tissue.
Function: absorbs shock and binds tissue together.
Provides structural support, flexibility, and protection to organs and tissues.

Supportive Connective Tissue

Supportive connective tissue is a type of connective tissue that provides strength, structure, and support to the body. It has a firm extracellular matrix that helps maintain the shape of organs and protects delicate structures.
Function: provides firmness, support, and structural integrity to the body, particularly in bone and cartilage.

Fluid Connective Tissue

Fluid connective tissue is a type of connective tissue that has a liquid extracellular matrix and functions in transport, immunity, and homeostasis.
Function: Provides firmness, support, and structural integrity to the body, particularly in bone and cartilage.
